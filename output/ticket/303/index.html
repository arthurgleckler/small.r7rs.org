[Based on feedback from Marc Feeley.]

delay and force were simple balanced concepts, but the introduction of lazy somewhat confuses the issue - when is delay appropriate and when is lazy? A simple solution would be to rename lazy to delay-force, indicating it is simply the composition of delay and force, and letting people see directly in code the balance of delays and forces.

description[Based on feedback from Marc Feeley.] delay and force were simple balanced concepts, but the introduction of lazy somewhat confuses the issue - when is delay appropriate and when is lazy? A simple solution would be to rename lazy to delay-force, indicating it is simply the composition of delay and force, and letting people see directly in code the balance of delays and forces. The eager procedure is named particularly unfortunately because it sounds as though it is in some way paired with lazy, and there is anecdotal evidence it was voted in on this misunderstanding. In fact, it is completed unrelated to lazy, being just a utility procedure that has never been seen used in practice. Perhaps a better name for it would be promise, since it just creates an (already computed) promise value.
